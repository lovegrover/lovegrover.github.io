1.1.1. The model

  1. Excluded volume:

    Excluded volume interaction between any two monomers is given by Lennard Jones potential. \[U_{LJ}(r)=\begin{cases} 4\epsilon \left[\left(\frac{\sigma}{r}\right)^{12}-\left(\frac{\sigma}{r}\right)^{6} \right]+\epsilon, & \text{for } r \leq 2^{1/6}\sigma \\ 0, & \text{for }r > 2^{1/6}\sigma \end{cases} \]

    • Here \(\sigma\) is the effective diameter of monomer.
    • \(\epsilon\) is the strength of potential

  2. Bonds property:

    The connectivity (polymer bond) between neighboring monomer is modeled as a Finitely Extensible Nonlinear Elastic (FENE) spring \[U_{FENE}(r)=-\frac{1}{2}kR_0^2\ln{(1-r^2/R_0^2)}\]

    • \(r\) distance between two consecutive monomers.
    • \(k\) is the spring constant

    • \(R_0\) is the maximum allowed seperation between connected monomers

      Figure 1: FENE potential and force

  3. Stiffness:

    \[U_{bend}(\theta_{i})=\kappa(1-\cos(\theta_i))\]

    • \(\theta_i\) is the complimentary angle between the bonds vector \(\vec{b}_{i-1}=\vec{r}_{i}-\vec{r}_{i-1}\) & \(\vec{b}_{i}=\vec{r}_{i+1}-\vec{r}_{i}\)
